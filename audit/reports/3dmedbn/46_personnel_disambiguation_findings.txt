================================================================
What was asked
================================================================

The user asked the BFR to disambiguate: confirm personnel are real
(not fabricated), identify who physically needs space in BN
facilities vs who works elsewhere (clinic, field, deployed).

================================================================
What was done
================================================================

1. Parsed the BFR TO sheet row by row.
2. Parsed the 3DMLG_ASR.xlsx '3D MED BN' sheet, which is a pivot
   of company subtotals plus per-MOS and per-grade aggregates.
3. Compared BFR (MOS, grade) counts against ASR (MOS, grade) at
   the company level.
4. Identified BFR rows with no Branch code as candidate
   non-billet rows.
5. Walked the TO sheet top to bottom maintaining a "current
   section" pointer to associate each real billet with the
   immediately preceding section header.
6. Bucketed each section by function (BN admin vs clinical/
   deployable vs TBD) using the section name only.

================================================================
Headline result
================================================================

The BFR TO sheet contains 710 rows. Of those:

  611 are real billets (have Grade, BMOS/PMOS, Branch)
   99 are organizational unit headers (Section / Platoon / Team
      titles in the T/O hierarchy; no Grade, no MOS, no Branch)

The 99 header rows had previously been miscounted as "unknown
billets". They are not billets. They are the org-structure rows
above each group of billets.

  ASR authoritative billet count:   609
  BFR real billet count:            611 (delta of 2, within data-vintage)
  CG signed letter personnel:       711
  BFR-to-CG delta:                  100 (likely augment / FAP / civilian /
                                        contractor not in ASR or per-co TFSMS)
  BFR-to-ASR delta:                   2 (negligible)

The CG-to-ASR 102-person gap is the same gap noted in the prior
session handoff. It is not 'fabrication'; it is structure that
exists in TFSMS or CG planning but is not authorized in the
master ASR for this unit. The exact source requires per-co TFSMS
extract reconciliation, held for the next session.

================================================================
MOS-level reconciliation (full detail in 46_asr_bfr_mos_pivot.txt)
================================================================

Marine MOS codes (4-digit): BFR matches ASR exactly for almost
every code in all three companies. Drift items: H&S has
1142+1, 1161+1, 1164-2, 2300+1, 3043+1, 3047-1, sum +1.

Navy officer MOS codes (2100, 2300, 2900): BFR matches ASR
exactly per company. Doctors and dentists fully accounted for.

Navy enlisted (L-code NEC specialties):

The BFR has COLLAPSED every Navy NEC specialty into a single
'L03A' bucket. ASR distinguishes L00A, L03A, L10A, L12A, L17A,
L22A, L23A, L24A, L31A. BFR shows only L03A.

  H&S:    BFR L03A=131, ASR L-codes sum=130 (+1)
  Surg A: BFR L03A=122, ASR L-codes sum=122 (exact)
  Surg B: BFR L03A=122, ASR L-codes sum=122 (exact)

Personnel counts match in aggregate. NEC specialty granularity
has been destroyed. This matters for clinical CCN sizing because
different L-code specialties (Independent Duty Corpsman L10A,
Field Medical Service Technician L17A, Submarine Medicine L12A,
etc.) drive different facility requirements. The BFR cannot
size a Series 500 clinical CCN correctly without restoring NEC
granularity from the per-co TFSMS source.

================================================================
Notes on method
================================================================

No fabricated numbers. Every count traces to a row in the BFR
TO sheet or a row in the ASR pivot. Bucket assignment is based
on section name only (the Billet Description text on the
header row); section names that are doctrinally ambiguous are
marked TBD per Apex Omega rule 4 rather than assigned.

The 99 organizational headers were not flagged in any prior
audit. They were counted as 'unknown personnel' in earlier
discussion. They are not personnel. They are T/O hierarchy.
