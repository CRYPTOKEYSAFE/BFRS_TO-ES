3d MED BN Camp Kinser Facility Plan

Predicate to the forthcoming MLG consolidation review. Controlled Unclassified Information.

Bottom line. The strategic basing decision has been made. 3d Medical Battalion is consolidating on Camp Kinser. The unit has physically relocated. The work now is facility planning and project development to support the relocation, leveraging existing Basic Facility Requirements data to identify gaps and surpluses, then producing a scope of work that can award between now and Q4 of FY27. This effort is the predicate to the broader 3d MLG consolidation review that follows.

Command Intent

Per CG 3d MLG signed letter

Consolidate 3d Medical Battalion on Camp Kinser. Co-locate Headquarters and Service Company, Alpha Surgical Company, Bravo Surgical Company, Motor Transport and Utilities, and the Medical Skills Training Center on one installation. Place the battalion adjacent to Medical Logistics Company, CLR-35, which already houses the deployable medical equipment used by 3d MED BN. Improve operational posture by reducing equipment-to-unit response time.

Source: CG 3d MLG signed letter (2 Feb 26) paras 1, 2, 3.

What This Work Is

Per Kenji Music email 1 May 26

The Strategic Basing Action (SBA) decision is complete. The primary goal of this effort is not to provide a supporting assessment for the SBA decision. The goal is the facility planning and project development necessary to properly support the relocation of 3d MED BN to Kinser. There is consensus on both the III MEF and MCIPAC sides that the facilities 3d MED BN has, and is in the process of relocating into, need work to convert and mitigate deficiencies. The list of specific facility requirements per building is expected to be substantial and may require a phased approach. Short-term gaps are closed first; longer-term requirements line up with the bigger MLG consolidation and modernization plans.

Source: Kenji Music email RE: CG MCIPAC Endorsement Request, 1 May 26 11:43 JST.

Why It Matters

Sourced

III MEF requires eight surgical platoons to meet wartime requirements. Per the CG 3d MLG signed letter dated 2 February 2026, III MEF is staffed to three surgical platoons within the First Island Chain. Bravo Surgical Company is part of the relocation back from Hawaii.

Per Tab A page 7 (3d MLG basing-action letter, 20 March 2025), the 2020 relocation of Bravo Surgical Company from Okinawa to Hawaii created unintended consequences for III MEF surgical readiness. The Hawaii-based company was increasingly tasked with supporting MCB Camp Smith, Tripler Army Medical Center, and Joint Base Pearl Harbor. Those are valid missions, but they are not III MEF missions, and they pulled surgical capacity away from where III MEF needs it.

Sources: CG 3d MLG signed letter para 1; Tab A page 7 paras 1 and 2.

Strategic Risk
DPRI POR retains only detachments
Per CG MCIPAC endorsement draft, the DPRI Pacific Posture Program of Record retains only task-organized detachments of Supply, Maintenance, and Medical Battalions on Okinawa long-term. The Kinser consolidation is operationally sound near term but in tension with the long-term POR. Mitigation is HQMC-level: engage DPRI to modify POR, or invest in new development areas such as KASA-1, CTA, or NTA.
Coordination Risk
ICG deconflict
Installation Communications Grid effort is recent and at early scope. Camp Kinser is identified among the more impacted sites. Facility work scopes need to coordinate against ICG cable routing and equipment placement once the grid scope firms up.

Future State Considerations

Captured for situational awareness

Per Mr. Kenji Music's planning view, downstream factors may reshape the 3d MED BN footprint over the medium term:

  • Once the Mega Warehouse completes, the H-block warehouses on Camp Foster open up, creating space MEDLOG could absorb at Foster.
  • Truck Company moves to Camp Courtney, freeing Motor T capacity at Foster.
  • Additional armory capacity is coming online.
  • Kinser Tower renovation, if it lands, delivers new converted admin facilities at Kinser.
  • Foster's adjacency to the hospital provides clinical sustainment access for medical units.

Taken together, these conditions could open a downstream path for 3d MED BN to return to Foster after the Kinser consolidation completes its near-term function. This is captured for situational awareness, not stated as plan. The current plan of record is the Kinser consolidation per the CG signed letter and the CG MCIPAC endorsement.

Source: client conversation, captured here as the client's view, not as basing action.
